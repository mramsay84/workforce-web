## Block 2: The Insight

**Section Label:** Why this is different

**Opening Diagnostic:** Every AI tool today has the same structural flaw: one agent doing everything.

**Expansion:** A single agent hits context limits on complex work. It cannot simultaneously specialize in legal reasoning, software architecture, and financial modeling — so it generalizes, and generalization is where quality falls apart. Worse: when one agent does everything, you become the coordinator. You route the tasks, you track the decisions, you catch the mistakes. That is not what AI was supposed to replace.

**The Pivot:** Workforce is different because it runs teams.

Instead of one generalist agent, every Workforce project runs a structured team: a planning consultant who designs the right approach, leads who own their domains, and specialists who execute in parallel — each scoped to what they actually know. Work is reviewed at every stage before it closes. No one does everything. No one skips the review.

**Visual Direction:** Two-panel diagram. Left: "One agent doing everything." Right: "A structured team — Planner, Leads, Specialists — each in their domain." Keep it to two elements. The contrast carries the message.

**Section Headline:** How it works

**Step 1 — Brief the platform**
Describe what needs to be done in plain language. No workflow configuration, no technical setup. You describe the work; the platform figures out how to staff it.

**Step 2 — Planner designs your team**
A planning consultant reviews your brief, selects the right leads and specialists for the project, and submits a structured execution proposal. Nothing starts until you approve the plan.

**Step 3 — Team executes in parallel**
Leads own their domains. Specialists work simultaneously across workstreams — no sequential bottleneck. Legal, financial, and research teams run at the same time. Engineering frontend and backend work in parallel.

Deliverables arrive structured and reviewed. High-stakes decisions and critical findings come back to you. The platform handles coordination, review cycles, and quality enforcement. You stay in the decision seat.

## Block 6: Cross-Mode Power

**Section Headline:** Some projects don't fit one domain. That's the point.

Complex work spans multiple domains. Workforce is designed to sequence them.

### Sequence 1: Research → Strategy → Marketing → Content → Website

Validate the market. Design the approach. Build the campaign. Write the copy. Ship the site. Each phase's deliverable becomes the next phase's brief — the same positioning, the same brand standards, carried through every workstream.

### Sequence 2: Compliance → Development

Audit the codebase. Identify findings, severity-rated. Then a Development sprint to fix what was found. Findings become the engineering brief. No gap between what was audited and what gets fixed.

### Sequence 3: Legal + Finance + Research → Due Diligence

Three parallel teams running simultaneously — legal risk review, financial model validation, market and competitive research. A synthesis lead assembles the integrated package. What takes a multi-firm team weeks runs in parallel workstreams.
