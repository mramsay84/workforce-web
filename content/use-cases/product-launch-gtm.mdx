<Section title="What agents produce">

Three coordinated teams, three sequential phases. Marketing produces the strategy — who to target, what to say, how to sequence. Content executes — launch posts, email sequences, social content. Website builds — campaign landing page, updated product pages, announcement page. All using the same messaging framework, the same audience insights, the same brand positioning.

</Section>

<Section title="Why sequential teams matter">

The most common marketing failure is building the site before the strategy is clear, writing the copy before the messaging is locked, or running these three phases simultaneously with no coordination.

Workforce enforces the right sequence: strategy first, then content execution, then web build. Each phase uses the prior phase's outputs as locked inputs. No rework when the messaging changes on day three.
