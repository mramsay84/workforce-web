<Section title="How a systematic literature review runs" variant="surface">
  <StepList steps={[
    {
      title: "You define the research question and scope",
      description: "Describe what you're trying to establish, the domain, any prior knowledge of the literature, and the decisions the review needs to support. The planner proposes the team and methodology framework."
    },
    {
      title: "Research Lead designs the methodology",
      description: "Before any search begins, the Research Lead documents the search strategy — databases, search terms, inclusion and exclusion criteria. The methodology is reviewed and locked before the literature search starts."
    },
    {
      title: "Literature search and evidence grading",
      description: "Literature Search Specialists run the documented search, retrieve sources, and begin the evidence table. The Evidence Grading Specialist grades each source as it comes in — type, recency, methodological quality."
    },
    {
      title: "Hypothesis tracking throughout collection",
      description: "As evidence accumulates, supporting and contradicting citations are logged against each hypothesis. Nothing is resolved prematurely — the tracking log reflects the state of the evidence at every point."
    },
    {
      title: "Think Tank critique phase",
      description: "Before synthesis begins, agents present competing interpretations and challenge each other's reading of the evidence. Gaps in coverage are flagged. Weak evidence dressed up as strong consensus is called out. Critique closes before the Synthesis Lead begins."
    },
    {
      title: "Synthesis with confidence ratings",
      description: "The Synthesis Lead integrates findings from the evidence table, hypothesis tracking log, and critique phase. Conclusions carry confidence ratings. Where evidence is mixed or limited, the synthesis says so explicitly."
    }
  ]} />
</Section>
