### Hero Use Case 4: NDA Triage at Scale

**Domain:** Legal

**Headline:** 12 NDAs. Three triage tiers. You review only what needs your attention.

**Story:** Submit a batch of NDAs. Specialist agents review each one independently — clause-by-clause — applying GREEN/YELLOW/RED risk tiering. A lead reviews edge cases and escalations. You receive a structured triage report: which NDAs are clear, which need negotiation, which require immediate attention. No queue. No sequential wait.

**What gets built:**
- Per-NDA clause-by-clause analysis with risk tiers
- GREEN / YELLOW / RED classification for each agreement
- Negotiation recommendations for YELLOW items
- Escalation flags for RED items — flagged for mandatory human review
- Consolidated triage report: status, priority actions, key risks

## Cross-Mode Projects Callout

**Section Headline:** Some projects span multiple domains. That's the point.

Workforce is designed to sequence work modes into coordinated multi-phase projects. The same brief, brand standards, and context carry forward across every phase.

### Marketing → Content → Website
Strategy first. Then copy. Then the site. One coherent project — from positioning to published page — without the handoff gaps that fragment strategy from execution.

### Research → Strategy → Development
Validate the market. Design the approach. Then build. Research findings become the strategic brief. Strategy becomes the engineering spec. No gap between what was learned and what gets built.

### Compliance → Development
Audit first. Findings, severity-rated. Then a Development sprint to fix what was found. The audit becomes the engineering brief. No translation layer. No lost context.

**Framing Note:** Cross-mode sequencing means each phase's deliverable becomes the next phase's input. User-initiated project starts are required between phases. We describe this as "designed to sequence," not as a single automatically-chained pipeline.
